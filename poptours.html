Zip-lining through lush rainforests

One of the most thrilling ways to explore Costa Rica’s rainforests is by soaring above the canopy on a zip-line. These adventures offer a bird’s-eye view of the lush landscapes and often include hanging bridges and observation towers.

Zip-line tours are available throughout the country, with popular locations near Arenal Volcano, Monteverde Cloud Forest, and Manuel Antonio. Most tours include professional guides, safety equipment, and optional extras like Tarzan swings or rappelling.

Typical Price: $50–$90 USD per person

Top Zip-line Parks in Costa Rica:

Sky Adventures (Arenal & Monteverde) Selvatura Park (Monteverde) Vista Los Sueños (Jacó)

White Water Rafting Adventures

Costa Rica is a paradise for white water rafting, offering rivers for both beginners and thrill-seekers. The most popular rivers include the Pacuare, Reventazón, and Sarapiquí, known for their stunning scenery and challenging rapids.

Tours usually include safety gear, experienced guides, and a delicious lunch by the river. Whether you're after adrenaline or a scenic float, there's something for everyone.

Typical Price: $65–$120 USD per person

Recommended Rafting Companies:

Costa Rica Rios (Pacuare & Pejibaye) Exploradores Outdoors (Pacuare River) Tico’s River Adventures (Sarapiquí)

Canyoning and Waterfall Rappelling

For those who crave an adrenaline rush, canyoning—also known as waterfall rappelling—is a must-try adventure in Costa Rica. This exciting activity involves hiking, climbing, and rappelling down waterfalls deep in tropical canyons.

Tours typically take place in areas like Arenal, Manuel Antonio, and Turrialba, where lush forests and dramatic terrain make for an unforgettable experience. No prior experience is needed, and professional guides ensure safety and fun for all participants.

Typical Price: $75–$120 USD per person

Top Canyoning Tours in Costa Rica:

Pure Trek Canyoning (Arenal) Desafío Adventure Company (La Fortuna) Jaco Adventure Park (Jacó)

ATV Tours through Costa Rica's wild terrain

If you're craving excitement and a bit of mud, ATV tours are a thrilling way to explore Costa Rica's rugged backroads, hills, and jungle trails. These all-terrain adventures offer a chance to discover hidden waterfalls, cross rivers, and climb mountain paths — all while riding your own ATV.

Tours are available for both beginners and experienced riders, and most include a safety briefing, equipment, and a local guide who knows the terrain. Popular areas for ATV rides include Jacó, Tamarindo, Manuel Antonio, and the Arenal region.

Typical Price: $65–$110 USD per person

Top ATV Tour Providers in Costa Rica:

AXR Jaco Tamarindo ATV Tours Vista Los Sueños Adventure Park (Jacó)

Horseback Riding to Waterfalls

Discover Costa Rica's natural beauty in a truly traditional way — on horseback. These guided tours take you along scenic trails through lush forests and farmlands, often leading to hidden waterfalls where you can swim and relax.

Suitable for all experience levels, horseback tours are a peaceful yet adventurous way to connect with the landscape. Many tours also include cultural elements, such as visits to local villages or traditional Costa Rican ranches.

Typical Price: $40–$70 USD per person

Recommended Horseback Tour Operators:

La Carolina Lodge (Near Tenorio Volcano) Horse Trek Monteverde Vista Los Sueños (Jacó)

Jungle Sightseeing Tours

Discover the raw beauty of Costa Rica’s tropical rainforests on a jungle sightseeing tour. These immersive experiences take you deep into the heart of the jungle, where you’ll encounter exotic wildlife, towering trees, hidden waterfalls, and vibrant flora.

Jungle tours can be done by foot, boat, or even on aerial trams. Guides will help you spot monkeys, sloths, toucans, frogs, and more, while sharing insights into the ecology and conservation of these rich ecosystems.

Typical Price: $25–$80 USD per person

Popular Jungle Tours in Costa Rica:

MIX & MATCH Arenal Park Jungle Tours(La Fortuna, San Carlos) Jungle Life Costa Rica (Bijagua)

Scuba Diving & Snorkeling

Costa Rica's warm Pacific and Caribbean waters offer vibrant marine life, coral reefs, and excellent visibility, making it a paradise for scuba divers and snorkelers.

Top destinations include Isla del Caño, Bat Islands, and Cahuita National Park. Whether you're a certified diver or a curious beginner, guided tours provide equipment, safety briefings, and expert instruction.

Typical Price: $45–$130 USD per person

Top Dive & Snorkeling Locations:

Caño Divers (Drake Bay) Scuba Diving Costa Rica (Bat Islands) Cahuita National Park (Snorkeling)

Surfing

Costa Rica is a world-renowned surfing destination, offering consistent waves, warm waters, and surf spots for every skill level. From the Pacific to the Caribbean, surfers flock here year-round for the perfect ride.

Top surf towns like Tamarindo, Santa Teresa, and Puerto Viejo are known for their laid-back vibe, surf schools, board rentals, and beachside cafés. Whether you’re a beginner or a pro, there’s a wave with your name on it.

Typical Price: $20–$60 USD for board rental or group lesson

Best Surfing Spots in Costa Rica:

Witch’s Rock Surf Camp (Tamarindo) Lapoint Surf Camp Caribbean Surf School (Puerto Viejo)

Kayaking Tours

Kayaking in Costa Rica is a peaceful and scenic way to explore the country’s diverse ecosystems — from tranquil lakes to winding mangrove tunnels and ocean coastlines. These tours are great for both beginners and experienced paddlers.

Popular kayaking spots include Lake Arenal, Damas Island mangroves near Manuel Antonio, and Golfo Dulce in the south. Many tours also offer wildlife watching opportunities, especially for birds and monkeys.

Typical Price: $40–$80 USD per person

Top Kayaking Locations:

Damas Island Mangrove Kayak Tour Kayak Jaco (Pacific Coast) Desafio Adventures (Lake Arenal)
