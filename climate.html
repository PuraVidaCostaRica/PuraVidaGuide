Costa Rica has a tropical climate with two distinct seasons: the dry season (known locally as "verano") and the rainy season ("invierno"). The dry season typically lasts from December to April, while the rainy season runs from May to November.

The climate varies depending on the region. The Pacific Coast tends to be hot and dry during the dry season, while the Caribbean side remains more humid and receives rainfall throughout the year. The Central Valley, where the capital San José is located, enjoys a mild and spring-like climate year-round.

Despite the name, the rainy season doesn't mean constant rain — most days start sunny with showers in the afternoon or evening. This period is also known as the "green season" due to the lush vegetation.

Travelers can enjoy Costa Rica year-round, but the best time to visit depends on the activities they plan and the regions they want to explore.
