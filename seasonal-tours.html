Whale Watching

Whale watching in Costa Rica is a breathtaking experience, especially along the southern Pacific coast where humpback whales migrate twice a year. From July to October and again from December to April, these gentle giants can be seen breaching and playing in the warm tropical waters.

The best spots to catch a glimpse are Uvita (home to the Marino Ballena National Park) and the Golfo Dulce, where calm waters offer perfect viewing conditions.

Sport Fishing Season

Whether you're chasing marlin in the Pacific or reeling in tarpon on the Caribbean side, Costa Rica offers world-class sport fishing all year round ‚Äî with peak seasons for different species. The Pacific coast shines from December to April for marlin and sailfish, while May to November offers incredible inshore action. The Caribbean side boasts legendary tarpon runs, especially from September to November. With expert guides, stunning coastal scenery, and thrilling action on the line, it's an unforgettable experience for anglers of all levels.

  • Locations: Pacific coast (Quepos, Tamarindo, Golfito), Caribbean coast (Tortuguero, Barra del Colorado)
  • Seasons: Vary by species and region (see above)
  • Highlights: Big game fishing, expert crews, catch-and-release options
