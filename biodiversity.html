Costa Rica is one of the most biodiverse countries on Earth, home to nearly 6% of the world’s total species despite occupying only 0.03% of the planet’s surface. This small country boasts an incredible variety of ecosystems, including tropical rainforests, cloud forests, mangroves, wetlands, and coral reefs.

There are over 500,000 species living in Costa Rica, including iconic animals such as sloths, toucans, monkeys, jaguars, and colorful frogs. The marine life is equally impressive, with sea turtles, dolphins, and humpback whales often spotted off the coasts.

More than 25% of Costa Rica's land is protected in national parks and reserves. Some of the most famous include Corcovado National Park, Tortuguero National Park, and Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve.

This strong commitment to conservation has made Costa Rica a global leader in eco-tourism, drawing travelers who want to experience nature in its purest form.
